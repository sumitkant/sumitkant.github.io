Coherence: The key to unlocking full brain potential

  • Nov 26, 2022
  • Neuroscience, Self Improvement

I recently came across this ted talk, which turned out to be an interesting lecture, that sometimes you wish all lectures were like. It turned out to be 2 ted talks of combined 44 minutes. I would recommend you to watch, but if you don't have enough time, continue reading. :)

TL;DR version

Dr. Alan Watkins, a Neuroscientist, has studied brain function for over a decade and identified a clear link between your Physiology (your body) and brain function. By measuring heart rate variance (HRV) - a measure of fluctuation of your heart beat over time - over long periods (hours,days), one can predict, when you are going to die and how much energy your body generates througout the day.

He alluded that, we feel depleted not because we have much to do and less time, but we have limited energey based on physiology. Having a coherent HRV is key to attaining energy levels of 10 years back. And this can be achieved though breathing smoothly & rhythmically through the heart area. This is most useful for senior leaders, who have to manage their energy under high stress environments.

Breathe. What?


I know you are like, this is what we involuntraliy do... but the point here is that breathing easily allows you to take control of your physiology. Let me tell why...

For performance in any area (academics, sports, business), we need to maintain it everyday for consistent results. Performance is maintained by following consistent behaviors. The behaviors and results are what lies externally.

What is internal is much deeper. Your behavior externally is impacted by what you think, and how well you think it. That thinking is deeply connected to how you feel. Thinking and feeling share a reciprocal relationship but mostly it is in the direction of feeling influencing thinking. How are you feeling is affected by your emotional state, what is generally considered as similar to feeling but it is not. Emotions are combination of signals (electrical, electromagnetic, chemical) from the different parts of your body. Which in turn is affected by your Physiology, which is nothing but streams of data coming from different organs of the body
