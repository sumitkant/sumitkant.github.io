Early Beginnings

Leonardo was born on April 15, 1452, in the Tuscan hill town of Vinci.

Mother

Siblings

Leonardo ended up with twelve half - brothers and sisters .

Education

Leonardo received an adequate education, learning basic Latin, mathematics, and geography. He was never a stellar student, instead finding his attention on things other than what were found in classrooms.

Italian Renaissance

The Black Death, which had ravaged Europe earlier in the fourteenth century, set the stage for the transition from the Middle Ages to a time of rebirth; hence the name Renaissance. The Italian Renaissance is best known for its art and architecture as well as a new philosophy. Renaissance humanism did not recognize any differences between sciences and the arts.

Mirror Writing

One of the things Leonardo loved to do was mirror - writing. Because he was left - handed, he would write from right to left on a page, with each letter written in reverse. He used this method throughout his life.

Verrocchio

Verrocchio was one of the best - known artists in all of Florence. His name, Verrocchio, meaning “true eye”

Apprenticeship

Leonardo became Verrocchio's apprentice. He was joined by Pietro Perugino, who painted the fresco known as The Delivery of the Keys in Rome’s Sistine Chapel. Aslo by Sandro Botticelli, who painted The Birth of Venus 20 years later.

Military Engineering

Leonardo informed Ludovico that he was quite knowledgeable about military engineering, come to stay in Milan for 17 years in the duke’s employ. Leonardo sketched war machines, pictures such as a war chariot with scythe blades mounted on both sides, to resemble a winged weapon; an armored tank which was propelled by two men cranking a shaft; and also a giant crossbow which required a number of men to operate

“I thought I was learning to live; I was only learning to die.”

The Mona Lisa

He often never finished what he started. In 1503, Leonardo would begin work on what has come to be known as his most famous painting of all: the Mona Lisa. The Mona Lisa has no discernible eyebrows or eyelashes. This was a custom of the time, as gentlewomen of the age considered these hairs unsightly. The painting was insured for over $782 million in 2015, and if you are ever in Paris, France, be sure to visit the Louvre museum where the esteemed Mona Lisa resides.

“A good painter has two chief objects to paint — man, and the intention of his soul”

Leonardo's Observations

Over the years, Leonardo kept notes and drawings that amounted to over 13,000 pages. Being left - handed, Leonardo wrote in mirror - image. Leonardo’s themes included painting, architecture, mechanics, and human anatomy.

Leonardo’s love of anatomy

Leonardo dissected animals, everything from monkeys, birds, and cows to frogs, bears, and horses. He compared his findings with those of their human counterparts.

sky is blue

Leonardo da Vinci could explain why the sky is blue. “The blue which is seen in the atmosphere is not its own color, but it is caused by the heated moisture having evaporated into the most minute imperceptible particles, which the beams of solar rays attract and cause to seem luminous against the deep intense darkness.”

Giorgio Vasari

Much of what we know about Leonardo da Vinci today is thanks to a little - known artist who lived in the middle of the sixteenth century, Giorgio Vasari. This man decided he could better make a difference by documenting the lives of the great Italian painters who came before him.

Giorgio Vasari Wikipedia
Information Overload

Leonardo suffered from his own information overload — but in a good way. Because he took what he knew and always improved on it, his genius set a standard that many today still grapple with.

The only problem there could possibly be with Leonardo da Vinci is that most of his original work was never finished or has been lost to time. Because he worked on so much at once, he rarely completed his projects.

polymath

someone whose talents run in all directions to the outer reaches of human intellect

He lived this motto to the fullest, ever learning, ever searching, never letting a moment of his life go by to waste.
