scenius

Think Process, Not Product

A lot of people are so used to just seeing the outcome of the work. They never see the side of the work you go through to produce the outcome

Take poeple behind the scenes

Audiences not only want to stumble across great work, but they too, long to be creative and be part of the creative process. By letting go of our egos and sharing our process, we allow for possibiility of people having an ongoing connection with us and our work, which helps us move more of our product.

become a documentarian of your work

Whether you share it or not, documenting and recording your process as you go along will make you feel like you are making progress and then when you are ready you'll have a surplus of material to choose from

send out daily dispatch

Stock & Flow

It is an economic concept. Flow is the feed. It's the posts and the tweets. Stock is the durable stuff. It is the content that you produce that's interesting. Stock is best made by collecting, organizing, and expanding upon your flow.

Don't be a hoarder

Your influences are all worth sharing as they clue people who you are and what you do - sometimes even more than your own work.

Where do you get your inspiration? What sorts of things do you fill your head with? What do you read? Do you subscribe to anything? What sites do you visit on the Internet? What music do you listen to? What movies do you see? Do you look at art? What do you collect? What’s inside your scrapbook? What do you pin to the corkboard above your desk? What do you stick on your refrigerator? Who’s done work that you admire? Who do you steal ideas from? Do you have any heroes? Who do you follow online? Who are the practitioners you look up to in your field?

Dumpster Diving

is one of the jobs of the artist—finding the treasure in other people’s trash, sifting through the debris of our culture, paying attention to the stuff that everyone else is ignoring

Influences

When you share your taste and your influences, have the guts to own all of it. Don’t give in to the pressure to self-edit too much.

Credit is always due

if you fail to properly attribute work that you share, you not only rob the person who made it, you rob all the people you've share with

Attribution

Another form of attribution that we often neglect is where we found the work that we’re sharing

The most important form of attribution is a hyperlink pointing back to the website of the creator of the work

Credit or Don't Share

Don’t share things you can’t properly credit. Find the right credit, or don’t share

Structure of fairy tale

talk about yourself at parties

Just because you are trying to tell a story doesn't mean you're inventing fiction. Stick to nonfiction. Tell the truth and tell it with dignity and self-respect.

Avoid Adjectives

Bios are not the place to practice creativity. Strike all the adjectives from your bio. If you take photos, you’re not an “aspiring” photographer, and you’re not an “amazing” photographer, either. You’re a photographer.

Don’t get cute. Don’t brag. Just state the facts.
